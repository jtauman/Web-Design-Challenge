The data generated from this project indicate that the temperature is generally the lowest at the North and South Poles (80 and -80 degrees respectively) and as one approaches the equator (latitude = 0 degrees) from the poles, the temperature increases. Interesting, the Northern Hemisphere had peak temperatures for cities between 20-40 degrees latitude, which is likely due to those cities experience their summer temperature ranges at the time of the query.

No reltionships were observed between % humidity, % cloudiness or windspeed with latitude. These results indicate that these weather paramaters are influenced more heavily by local weather systems and geographical features rather than relative position to the equator.
